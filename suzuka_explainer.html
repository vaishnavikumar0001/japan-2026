01 / 10 — The Series

What Is Super Formula?

Japan's premier single-seater racing series — and arguably the second-fastest formula series in the world after F1. These cars lap Suzuka faster than GP2, faster than IndyCar, and within touching distance of 2010-era F1 machinery.

02 / 10 — The Track

Suzuka — The Legend

Built by Honda in 1962 as a test track. The world's only figure-eight circuit — cars cross over themselves between the Crossover Bridge. Every serious F1 driver ranks Suzuka as their favourite circuit on the planet.

S-Curves
Turns 3–7
The iconic flowing esses. Drivers go flat out — any wobble ends in the wall. The most photographed sequence in motorsport.
Degner Curve
Turns 11–12
Blind high-speed right. Named after a rider who crashed here in 1962. Haunts drivers — the outside wall is unforgiving.
130R
Turn 15
300+ km/h flat-out left. Some drivers lift. Most don't. You feel the bravery from the grandstands. The bravest corner in the world.
Spoon Curve
Turns 13–14
Wide, fast double-apex. A great overtaking spot. Where championship-deciding passes have happened. Best general admission view.

06 / 10 — The Pit Walk

The Pit Walk
Experience

Walking the Suzuka pitlane with actual race cars present is one of the most exciting things a motorsport fan can do. Here's exactly what to expect and how to make the most of it.

🎫
The Ticket
Pit Walk passes are sold at the gate on the day — until 15 minutes after the Pit Walk starts. They sell out. Buy yours the moment you arrive at 1:30 PM. Do not browse the Honda Gallery first.
🚫
No Umbrellas
Umbrellas are NOT allowed in the pitlane — a safety rule. If rain is forecast, bring a compact poncho or waterproof jacket instead. The pit lane is narrow and umbrellas cause accidents.
📸
Photo Tips
Get low for car shots — the SF23 is tiny and low to the ground. Wide angle for team garage shots. The Dallara SF23 cockpit is incredibly small — photograph next to a mechanic for scale.
👀
What to Look For
Tyre blankets keeping the rubber warm. Engineers reviewing data on laptops. The tiny, angular SF23 bodywork — see how different it is from anything you've driven. Listen for engine warmup sounds.
🔍
Nerd Detail to Look For
The SF23 uses a push-rod suspension front and rear — the opposite of most modern F1 cars. The rear-mounted steering wheel camera position. The exposed Yokohama tyre compounds — soft compounds will be bright yellow-marked sidewalls. If mechanics are changing setups, watch how many people it takes to adjust wing angles — Super Formula cars are complex machines.
07 / 10 — Race Day Basics

How to Read a Race

Super Formula races are strategic puzzles as much as driving contests. Here's what to watch for at each stage so nothing surprises you.

The Start
Lap 1
Five red lights extinguish one by one — then they ALL go out simultaneously. Watch Turn 1 immediately. The pack bunches up — side-by-side, wheel-to-wheel. Most drama happens in the first corner.
Pit Stops
Laps 10-20
Mandatory tyre change. Cars peel into pitlane at 80 km/h, stop for about 3-4 seconds (Super Formula crews are fast), then rejoin. Track position changes dramatically here.
DRS Zones
Main Straight
Drag Reduction System — the rear wing opens on the main straight to give the chasing car a speed boost. If a car is within 1 second of the car ahead at detection point, it gets DRS. Enables overtaking on the straight.
Safety Car
After incidents
All cars queue behind the Safety Car. Gaps close up — restarts are explosive. Some teams use this to pit without losing position. Watch strategy unfold in real time.
Flags
Key signals
Yellow = incident ahead, no overtaking. Blue = faster car behind, let them pass. Red = race stopped. Chequered = race over. Black/white = driving standards warning.
The Podium
Race end
Top 3 park up in Parc Ferme. Celebrations happen in front of a dedicated podium. Super Formula podiums are animated — fans at the fence, teams pouring in. Stay for it.
🎧
Team radio is broadcast in Japan. If you have a Japanese radio app or pick up an English broadcast at the circuit, listen for team/driver communication during safety car periods — this is when strategy decisions get made and the conversation gets intense.
08 / 10 — Must-See Attractions

Suzuka Circuit Park

Beyond the race itself, Suzuka Circuit Park is a full motorsport destination. Here's what's worth your time — ranked by priority for a motorsport fan visiting in May 2026.

🏛️
Honda Racing Gallery
Priority 1 — FREE with entry
The centrepiece. Open January–end of May 2026. Features Ayrton Senna's actual 1990 McLaren MP4/5B F1 machine (the car that won the 1990 World Championship). Also Valentino Rossi's RC211V Honda MotoGP motorcycle, stripped down to show the internal engineering. F1 power units from Honda's championship-winning years on display. Photography allowed — absolutely post this on social media.
⚡ Go here before the pit walk — it closes based on crowd levels
🎡
Circuit Ferris Wheel
Priority 1 — Go FIRST on arrival
The tallest structure in Mie Prefecture. From the top you see the entire figure-eight circuit layout — the crossover bridge, the main straight, the S-curves, the infield. The only place you can truly comprehend the full track geography. During race sessions you can hear the cars from up here. Best photo of the day. Early afternoon visit = shorter queues.
🎯 Ride before 2 PM — queues build significantly later
🏁
130R Corner View
Priority 2 — Best spectating spot
Standing at the outside of 130R with a Super Formula car going flat-out past you at 300+ km/h is genuinely one of the most physically intense motorsport experiences available to a spectator. The noise arrives before the car does. General admission access — no reserved seat needed. Get here 20 min before sessions start.
👂 Cover your ears for the first few cars until you calibrate
🎮
Racing Theater
Priority 2 — Interactive museum
Interactive motorsport education centre. Bike technology from A to Z. Reaction time simulators — try the same mental response tests that real racing drivers train with. The "Legends of Suzuka" exhibit covers every major championship-deciding race held here. Air-conditioned — great if it rains or gets hot.
🌧️ Perfect backup if weather turns bad
🛒
Official Merch & Paddock Shop
Priority 3 — Shop between races
Super Formula team merchandise, car models, diecast replicas, driver items, circuit-branded goods. Japan-exclusive items you cannot get anywhere else. Prices are very reasonable compared to F1 equivalents. Buy during the gap between Race 1 finish and Race 2 — queues are shorter during on-track action.
💡 Buy merch during Race 2 if you're watching on screens — least crowded
🏎️
Go-Kart Experience
Priority 4 — Time permitting
Circuit Challenger — drive an electric kart on part of the actual Suzuka track. Lewis Hamilton did his early training here. Separate ticket required on top of park pass. Genuinely worth it if you have 45 minutes to spare before your return bus.
⏰ Only if you have 45+ min before your 17:45 departure
09 / 10 — Practical Tips

Make It Perfect

Suzuka race days reward preparation. These are the things that separate a great visit from a frustrating one.

🎫
Buy Pit Walk Pass FIRST. The moment you enter the gate, go straight to the Pit Walk ticket point before anything else. They stop selling 15 minutes after the walk begins and they sell out. Do not see the Ferris wheel first — pit walk ticket first, everything else second.
🚌
Kintetsu return ticket — already bought. You pre-purchased both directions at Nagoya Station in the morning. Your return is already secured. Leave at 17:45 to beat the post-race crush at Shiroko shuttle bus stop.
🌧️
No umbrellas in the pitlane. If rain is forecast, a compact waterproof poncho goes in your daypack. Japanese konbini (7-Eleven / Lawson) near Shiroko Station sell ponchos cheaply if caught unprepared.
📷
Camera lens limit: 26cm. Longer lenses are not permitted at Suzuka events. A 70-200mm zoom at 26cm is fine. Phone cameras are unrestricted. Monopods are permitted, tripods are not.
💴
Carry cash. Some merchandise stalls and food vendors at the circuit prefer cash — card acceptance is improving but not universal at Japanese motorsport events. ¥3,000-5,000 in cash is advisable beyond the Suica card.
🍱
Food at the circuit. Over 20 restaurants and stalls inside. The circuit bento boxes are good value and Japan-themed. Ramen, yakisoba, takoyaki, curry — all available. Eat during the gap between Race 1 and Race 2 when queues are shorter.
📱
Check session times the night before. At suzukacircuit.jp — exact race start times for Round 4 and Round 5 will be confirmed closer to the event. The schedule we have is approximate — real-world timing can shift by 15-30 minutes.
🏁
Best grandstand on a budget. General admission near Spoon Curve (West Area included with Race Day Special Passport). Excellent overtaking view. Alternatively, stand at 130R exit — hear the commitment from every driver on every lap.
